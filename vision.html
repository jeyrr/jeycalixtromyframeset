About Skye

Skye is derived from the Norse word ski meaning ‘cloud’ and ey, ‘island’. Designer Alexander McQueen’s ashes were scattered on Skye. Twelve of the peaks in the Black Cuillin , Skye’s famous mountain range, are munros. One of the island’s most famous visitors was Bonnie Prince Charlie, who arrived in 1745 disguised as a serving maid.
